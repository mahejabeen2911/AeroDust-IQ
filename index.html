Unoptimized Baseline

Step 1: The Dual-Source Dust Problem

Heavy mineral haulers on the Raipur-Bhilai highway act as major environmental hazards due to two distinct physical phenomena:

  • Wind Erosion Spillage: At highway speed, wind shears across open truck beds, blowing off fine coal dust or fly ash directly into the air (spilling up to 18.7 kg per 100 km).
  • Low-Pressure Wake Suction: The blunt rear profile creates a vacuum zone behind the truck. This low pressure acts like a giant vacuum cleaner, lifting road silt (road dust) directly off the unpaved bypass paths.
Intervention 1: Cover Active

Step 2: Automated Cargo Cover (-97% Spillage)

The first step focuses on enclosing the cargo to prevent direct wind shear erosion:

  • PVC Tarpaulin ($650\text{ g/m}^2$): A heavy-duty polymer shield deploys dynamically over the bed once the vehicle speeds exceed $20\text{ km/h}$.
  • Eliminating Spillage: Stops direct cargo blow-off, dropping it from $0.187\text{ kg/km}$ to $<0.005\text{ kg/km}$ (saving approximately $18.5\text{ kg}$ of raw material per truck on a Raipur-Bhilai round trip).
Intervention 2: Aero Active

Step 3: Aerodynamic Boat-Tails (-42% Wake Lift)

Next, we tackle the massive air pressure vacuum created behind the flat tailgate:

  • Active Flaps: Dual-panel boat tails automatically extend to a optimized $15^\circ-20^\circ$ angle using electronic linear actuators.
  • Vortex Control: The flaps direct passing air into the low-pressure pocket. This eliminates the "suction vacuum" pulling road dust up into the air, reducing resuspended PM10 by **$-42\%$**.
  • Fuel Savings: Reduces aerodynamic drag coefficient ($Cd$) from $0.65$ to $0.48$, translating into **$+6.2\%$ fuel savings** on high-speed bypasses.
Intervention 3: Mist Active

Step 4: Smart Mist Curtain (-82% Dust Captured)

For any road dust that still manages to escape, a pressurized micro-droplet curtain knocks it down:

  • PM Coalescence: 8x tailgate nozzles project a fine $30\text{ }\mu\text{m}$ water-mist. These micro-droplets collide with fine dust particles, binding and making them heavy enough to settle instantly on the road.
  • Adaptive Flow Control: Solenoid valves adjust the water flow between $0.5\text{ L/min}$ to $3.0\text{ L/min}$ depending on the vehicle's speed and road dust levels, preventing water wastage in clean regions.
